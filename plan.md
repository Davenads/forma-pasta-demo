# Forma Pasta Cafe & Market — Demo Site Plan

## Business Profile

**Name:** Forma Pasta Cafe & Market
**Type:** Artisan fresh pasta kitchen, cafe, and retail market
**Address:** 531 Beaver Street, Sewickley, PA 15143
**Phone:** (412) 586-7195
**Instagram:** @formapastapgh
**Current Website:** formapgh.com (Wix / Thunderbolt renderer)
**Third-party ordering:** Toast Tab (online orders), OpenTable, Yelp
**Hours:** Lunch Tue–Fri 8am–4pm | Brunch Sat–Sun 8am–2pm | Dinner Fri–Sat 5–9pm
**BYOB:** Yes
**Special offerings:** Fresh pasta-making classes, in-house made pasta and sauces, market items to take home

**What makes Forma Pasta distinct:**
- All pasta and sauces made in-house, fresh daily
- BYOB — removes a friction point for casual dining
- Pasta classes are an experiential offering with no real online presence to support them
- The market angle — take-home fresh pasta and sauces — is a retail channel with zero visibility
- Limited hours and small space create scarcity/demand (Fri–Sat dinner is the destination window)
- Artisan, maker-first identity that current platforms cannot convey

## Current Web Presence Audit

**Platform:** Wix (Thunderbolt renderer v1.17230.0)
**Grade: C — Functional but thin**

**What the site does:**
- Built on Wix with modern responsive framework
- Clean minimal aesthetic, grid-based layout
- Image galleries with carousel/zoom controls
- Mobile-optimized with device-specific layouts
- Basic navigation with dropdown support
- Form elements and button components

**What it fails to do:**
- **No identity differentiation** — the Wix site reads as a generic restaurant template; the handmade, artisan, pasta-kitchen identity is not conveyed
- **Pasta classes are either buried or absent** — the single most differentiating experiential offering has no dedicated prominent feature
- **The market angle is invisible** — take-home fresh pasta and sauces are a retail revenue channel the current site does not surface
- **BYOB is not featured** — a meaningful differentiator that most competitors lack
- **No brand story** — who started Forma, why fresh pasta, what happens in the kitchen every morning before doors open
- **No photography that communicates craft** — Wix generic template does not showcase the artisan food production story

**The SEO problem still exists:**
Searching "Forma Pasta Sewickley" still surfaces formapasta.com (a Brooklyn restaurant) near the top, plus aggregator listings. The Wix site exists but does not clearly own its identity in search results.

**The pitch:** "You have a website. It doesn't look like your restaurant."

## Demo Objective

Build a brand-defining website from scratch that establishes Forma Pasta as THE artisan pasta destination in the Pittsburgh area. The demo should feel warm, handmade, and Italian-market inspired — a site that makes someone want to come in, take a class, and take fresh pasta home.

The demo has to do two things simultaneously: make the food irresistible and make the experience feel easy to access (hours, reservations, class booking, market).

- Date nights and casual but intentional dining occasions (BYOB angle appeals to these)

## Design Direction

**Aesthetic:** Artisan Italian market. Warm, handmade, tactile. Not rustic-kitschy — elevated craft. Think the visual language of a good Italian deli or a farm-to-table pasta brand: terracotta, wheat, cream, olive, with confident modern typography.

**Color palette:**
- Background: warm off-white / cream (#f7f2e8)
- Primary accent: terracotta (#c4622d)
- Secondary: deep olive / sage (#4a5a3a)
- Dark text: near-black warm brown (#1f1a14)
- Light accent: wheat/straw (#e8d5b0)

**Typography:**
- Headings: bold, slightly condensed serif — Playfair Display, Freight Display, or Canela
- Menu / item labels: clean light sans — Inter Light or Neue Haas Grotesk Light
- Accent / callout text: italic serif for warmth and personality

**Visual language:**
- Flour-dusted surfaces, pasta dough, hand-cut shapes
- Warm natural light food photography
- Market still-life: jars of sauce, fresh pasta hanging, vegetables
- Hand-drawn style accent elements (optional: thin sketched pasta shapes as decorative marks)

**Feel reference:** The visual brand of an Italian specialty importer or a quality pasta brand — similar to how Rustichella d'Abruzzo or a New York pasta shop presents itself online, applied to a neighborhood scale.

## Page Structure

### 1. Homepage
- **Hero:** Full-bleed image of fresh pasta being made or plated — warm light, hands in frame. Headline: "Made from scratch. Every day." or "Fresh pasta. Handmade in Sewickley."
- **Three pillars section:** Cafe / Classes / Market — three equally weighted callouts establishing the full scope of what Forma is
- **Featured menu item:** Rotating dish feature — "This week's pasta" — with a photo and one-line description
- **Classes callout:** "Learn to make fresh pasta from scratch" — book a class CTA
- **Market callout:** "Take it home" — fresh pasta and sauces available to go
- **Hours + BYOB note:** Clean hours block; "BYOB welcome" prominently displayed
- **Instagram strip:** Last 6 posts

### 2. Menu
- Three distinct sections: Breakfast/Lunch | Brunch | Dinner
- Italian-market visual language — styled like a printed menu card, not a table
- Key note: "All pasta made fresh in-house daily"
- BYOB callout: "Bring your favorite bottle — we'll provide the glasses"
- Pricing included for demo realism (sourced from Clover/Grubhub data)

### 3. Pasta Classes
- **Hero:** Photo of a class in session — hands, dough, flour
- What's included: what you learn, how long, group size, what you take home
- Booking interface: simple form or calendar widget (demo: mock calendar)
- Pricing and policies
- "Perfect for date nights, birthdays, team events" — occasion framing
- Testimonial or social proof (pull from Yelp reviews)

### 4. Market
- "Take the kitchen home" positioning
- Items available: fresh pasta varieties, house sauces, seasonal specials
- Market hours (are market items available during all cafe hours, or specific times?)
- "Call ahead to reserve your pasta for pickup" CTA
- Optional: email list for weekly fresh pasta availability notifications

### 5. About
- The story: who started Forma, why fresh pasta, what the kitchen philosophy is
- The space: small, intentional, neighborhood-rooted
- "Made in Sewickley" sense of place
- No fluff — specific and direct

### 6. Contact + Find Us
- Address, phone, hours
- Google Map embed
- "Reservations recommended for Fri/Sat dinner — call us or use OpenTable"
- Parking info

## Key Features to Build

1. **Three-pillar homepage section** — Cafe / Classes / Market as equal pillars with distinct CTAs. This immediately establishes Forma's full identity, which no current platform shows.

2. **Pasta class booking flow** — even as a demo mock, a functional-looking booking form with date selection, class size, and contact form is a powerful demo moment. Shows what a real booking flow could look like.

3. **Market section** — a clean product-listing style section for take-home pasta and sauces. Not e-commerce — just a menu/list of what's available with "call to reserve" or a simple order form.

4. **"This week's pasta" feature** — small homepage callout that rotates. Reinforces the fresh/seasonal identity and gives an excuse for repeat homepage visits.

5. **BYOB callout** — this is a real differentiator that is invisible on current platforms. It needs to be surface-level visible, not buried.

6. **Mobile-first cafe experience** — since many customers will check hours/menu from their phones while in Sewickley, mobile homepage and hours visibility are top priority.

## Tech Stack

- **Framework:** Next.js 14+ (App Router)
- **Styling:** Tailwind CSS
- **Animations:** Framer Motion (subtle reveals, not dramatic)
- **Images:** Next/Image with blur placeholders; warm-toned food stock from Unsplash (pasta, market, kitchen)
- **Fonts:** Google Fonts — Playfair Display + Inter, or Cormorant Garamond + Inter
- **Booking form:** React Hook Form + simple email send (Resend or Formspree for demo)
- **Deployment:** Vercel
- **CMS (production path):** Sanity for menu and class schedule updates; alternatively a simple JSON/markdown approach for the owner to manage themselves

## Content Strategy

**Headline options:**
- "Fresh pasta. Handmade in Sewickley."
- "Made from scratch. Every day."
- "The pasta kitchen in the heart of Sewickley Village."

**Voice:**
Warm but direct. Not precious or overly artisanal-posturing. The food speaks — the copy shouldn't oversell it. Short sentences. Specific ingredients and techniques over vague quality claims.

**About copy direction:**
Lead with what happens in the kitchen every morning (pasta being made before the doors open), not with the founders' bio. Make the reader feel the place before you tell them who built it.

**BYOB framing:**
Don't bury it in the FAQs. Put it on the homepage: "BYOB welcome. Bring your favorite bottle — we'll handle the rest."
